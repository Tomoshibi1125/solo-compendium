# The Glassline Claim

**A Rift Ascendant Introductory Mini-Adventure for Level 1 Ascendants**  
**Players:** 1-4  
**Runtime:** 3.5-5 hours  
**Campaign Use:** Lead-in to Run Silent without using Run Silent campaign mechanics  
**Primary Site:** Eternal Shadow Dungeon (`location-0001`), Rank D  
**Employer:** Ironclad Extraction Services  
**Party Role:** Licensed Ascendant escort/security  
**Source Authority:** Rift Ascendant sourcebooks in `books/exports` and canon compendium data

---

## Warden Overview

The Glassline Claim is a street-level Rift Ascendant contract about professional danger, field labor, salvage, and reputation. The characters are newly operating licensed Ascendants hired as escorts and bodyguards for an Ironclad Extraction Services mining/salvage crew entering the Eternal Shadow Dungeon, a permitted Rank-D Rift Interior.

This is not a rescue caused by an unprotected crew entering alone. The miners enter only because licensed Ascendants are escorting them. The job is ordinary by Rift Age standards: regulated, profitable, dangerous, and watched by people who care about liability, salvage rights, and who looks competent on an AFA log.

Run this like the first episode of a grounded action series: the party gets a clean first job, meets a memorable crew, shows who they are under pressure, clashes with a rival, survives a sharp final fight, and walks away with rewards that matter in the next campaign.

### Core Loop

1. Accept legal escort duty.
2. Cross a stable threshold with a work crew.
3. Keep noncombatant labor alive while they survey and extract.
4. Resolve a rival claim dispute without ruining the contract.
5. Defeat, drive off, distract, or outmaneuver the claim guardian.
6. Return with registered salvage, reputation, and a clean handoff hook.

### Canon Locks

- The Rift is not assumed to collapse, break, or destabilize.
- The Rift does not run on clocks, countdowns, or segmented pressure trackers.
- The mining crew enters only under licensed Ascendant escort.
- All permanent rewards are canon RA credits, items, favors, requisitions, or registered harvest.
- No campaign-region-specific lore appears.
- No Run Silent campaign-only mechanics appear.
- The Run Silent connection is a handoff hook, not a rules import.

## How To Run This One-Shot

This packet is written so a Warden can run from the page. Each scene includes read-aloud text, dialogue, player decisions, exit conditions, and a transition into the next scene.

### Runtime Targets

| Target | Use This Pace |
| --- | --- |
| 3.5 hours | Combine Scenes 1-2, shorten Scene 5, choose Standard Extraction in Scene 6, start the finale as soon as the seam is sampled. |
| 4 hours | Run all scenes, but keep each social scene to one main exchange and one check. |
| 5 hours | Let the party negotiate terms, investigate the rival claim, play out crew personalities, attempt legal harvest, and linger in debrief. |

### Pacing Checkpoints

| Table Time | Target Progress |
| ---: | --- |
| 0:30 | Contract accepted, party role clear, crew introduced. |
| 0:55 | Threshold crossed. |
| 1:40 | First combat resolved and route to L-3 identified. |
| 2:20 | Survey complete and rival pressure introduced. |
| 3:05 | Extraction method chosen and underway. |
| 3:40 | Final guardian active or about to reveal itself. |
| Final 20-30 min | Debrief, rewards, and Run Silent handoff. |

### Fast-Path Cuts

Use these when time is tight:

- Scene 1 and Scene 2 become one location: Mira briefs the party at the Bureau intake desk.
- Harlan Pike appears only as a claim tablet call instead of in person.
- Scene 4 requires two checks instead of three.
- Scene 6 uses Standard Extraction only.
- Skip legal harvest unless a player asks.

### Full-Path Options

Use these when the table has time:

- Let each PC get a short intro prompt before the contract is signed.
- Give Harlan's rival crew a worker who quietly dislikes his tactics.
- Let Dev's AFA feed show one true clue and one false reading.
- Let Nia negotiate salvage handling with a PC who seems practical.
- Let the final guardian damage the sample sled once before fully engaging.

### What Each Scene Teaches

| Scene | Teaches |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Ironclad Contract Briefing | Ascendants work in a legal economy, not only as dungeon fighters. |
| 2. Bureau Threshold Intake | Rift access is regulated, modern, and documented. |
| 3. First Corridor Escort | Formation, worker protection, light, and low-rank anomaly behavior matter. |
| 4. Survey Marker L-3 | Noncombat field work has stakes. |
| 5. Rival Claim Dispute | Not every obstacle is a monster. |
| 6. The Glassline Seam | Players choose risk level and consequences. |
| 7. Claim Guardian | The final fight is objective-based, not only HP attrition. |
| 8. Debrief And Carryover | Rewards, reputation, and clean records follow the party. |

## Episode Flow

Use this section for flavor and table rhythm. It adds no mechanics.

### Cold Open

Start with a quick image before explanation:

> White Bureau lamps burn through rain or dust over a fenced Rift Site. Workers wait behind yellow lines with sample cases at their boots. A black threshold hangs in the air like a door cut out of the world. On the AFA board, one contract blinks from pending to live:
>
> `BRO-D-GLASSLINE-L3 - LICENSED ASCENDANT ESCORT REQUIRED`

Then cut to the Ironclad briefing.

### Title Beat

After the party accepts the job, say:

> On the AFA feed, the contract name expands across the screen: **THE GLASSLINE CLAIM**.
>
> The crew looks at you for the first time like you are not applicants, not rookies, not names on a ledger. You are the reason they are allowed to step through the door.

### PC Spotlight Prompts

Ask each player one quick prompt before Scene 2:

- What detail on your gear tells people you are newly licensed?
- What does your AFA profile show when the Bureau clerk scans you?
- Who on the crew do you instinctively keep an eye on?
- When the threshold comes into view, do you look excited, careful, or annoyed that everyone is watching?

### Mid-Episode Escalation

Harlan Pike's arrival should feel like a rival stepping into frame, not a random interruption. He is not there to kill the party. He is there to test whether they understand the professional world they just joined.

### Final Reveal

The Claim Guardian should appear because the extraction has reached the seam's defended point, not because the Rift is breaking. The moment is about the party's job becoming real.

### Ending Stinger

After rewards, close on a small administrative detail:

> The AFA archive accepts the Glassline file. A green mark appears beside each of your names: **LOW-RANK ESCORT PERFORMANCE - RELIABLE**.
>
> Somewhere else in the Bureau system, that mark is copied into a contractor review queue for a future first-entry operation.

Do not reveal Run Silent-specific lore, region details, or mechanics here.

## Player Briefing

Read or paraphrase:

> Ironclad Extraction Services has a licensed Rank-D extraction slot inside the Eternal Shadow Dungeon. The Bureau has cleared the threshold for scheduled work crews, not public access. Ironclad has the labor, tools, and salvage permits. What it needs is licensed Ascendant protection.
>
> Your job is simple on paper: escort a four-person mining crew to Survey Marker L-3, protect them while they sample a glassy Essence seam, resolve any claim disputes by contract terms, and return through the same threshold with workers, samples, and AFA logs intact.
>
> Ironclad pays for clean work. The Bureau notices messy work. The Rift does not care either way.

### Contract Terms

- **Base Pay:** 1,000 credits per Ascendant.
- **Clean Escort Bonus:** +500 credits per Ascendant if all Ironclad crew return alive and ambulatory.
- **Documentation Bonus:** +500 credits per Ascendant if survey data, samples, and AFA logs are complete.
- **Salvage Rights:** party receives one Rank-D team salvage parcel after Ironclad's corporate samples are registered.
- **Legal Requirement:** no unregistered Relic claim, illegal Essence concealment, or violence against licensed workers.
- **Failure Clause:** pay can be reduced for preventable crew injury, ruined samples, unresolved claim violation, or Bureau complaint.

### What The Players Know

The Eternal Shadow Dungeon is a Rank-D Rift Interior known for shadow-stone corridors, false echoes, moving shadows, and low-rank anomaly bodies. It is used as a training-ground style operation for D-rank Ascendants and licensed work crews. Low rank does not mean harmless.

## Key NPCs

Use these NPCs as people first. They do not need full combat stat blocks.

### Mira Voss, Ironclad Claim Lead

- **Role:** contract officer outside the threshold.
- **Personality:** crisp, polite, already three problems behind schedule.
- **Wants:** a clean file, legal samples, and no Bureau inquiry.
- **Use At Table:** explains pay, claim rules, rival complications, and debrief consequences.
- **Voice:** "I do not need heroics. I need workers alive, samples sealed, and signatures where the Bureau expects them."
- **If Pressed:** "Yes, the rival claim is annoying. No, that does not make it illegal to proceed."

### Tomas Hale, Crew Boss

- **Role:** Construction Worker style field lead.
- **Gear:** hard hat, high-visibility vest, marked tool case, heavy wrench.
- **Wants:** keep the crew calm and finish the survey.
- **Useful Skill:** structural judgment. Can help identify unsafe floors if protected.
- **Vulnerability:** will not abandon a worker unless ordered by an Ascendant and given a reason.
- **Voice:** "You keep the shadows off us, we keep the tools steady. Everybody goes home with fingers and pay."

### Nia Cho, Rift Porter

- **Role:** logistics carrier and sample pack handler.
- **Gear:** reinforced expedition backpack, mining pick, durable coveralls, flashlight, sample ledger.
- **Wants:** get paid, avoid attention, keep samples properly labeled.
- **Useful Skill:** appraises anomaly parts, Essence fragments, and salvage quality.
- **Vulnerability:** if panicked, may try to save the sample pack before herself.
- **Voice:** "If it glows, hisses, whispers, or tries to be expensive, I put it in the ledger before anyone touches it."

### Dev Arlen, Survey Tech

- **Role:** AFA-linked surveyor and route marker.
- **Gear:** marker spikes, scanner tablet, chalk tape, disposable beacons.
- **Wants:** prove the L-3 seam is worth a future contract.
- **Useful Skill:** can provide advantage on one route or seam check if the party gives them a safe work window.
- **Vulnerability:** freezes when their AFA returns contradictory readings.
- **Voice:** "The map is arguing with itself. That is normal. I mean, not normal-normal. Rift normal."

### Rae Sutter, Junior Loader

- **Role:** new Ironclad laborer.
- **Gear:** hard hat, sample sled, coil straps.
- **Wants:** survive their first interior job and not look scared.
- **Useful Skill:** spots practical details others miss.
- **Vulnerability:** easiest crew member for anomalies or rivals to isolate.
- **Voice:** "I know the safety manual. I just did not expect the safety manual to have a smell."

### Harlan Pike, Rival Claim Agent

- **Role:** licensed representative of a competing extraction crew.
- **Personality:** smug, careful, contract-literate.
- **Wants:** delay Ironclad long enough to dispute the Glassline Seam.
- **Use At Table:** social obstacle, legal pressure, potential future rival.
- **Combat Note:** if violence starts, he orders withdrawal and files a complaint. Do not make this a murder scene unless the players force it.
- **Voice:** "Nobody here is stealing. We are all professionals. Some of us simply read the claim map before walking into a lawsuit."

## Running Escort Duty

The party's job is not only to kill anomalies. Their professional responsibilities create meaningful choices.

### Escort Priorities

- Keep the crew within line of sight when terrain allows.
- Put Ascendants between workers and unknown passages.
- Call a halt before investigating side routes.
- Secure work zones before tools come out.
- Decide when speed is worth risk.
- Preserve AFA logs and legal chain of custody.

### Crew Exposure

When the party ignores the crew, splits too far, starts a fight in the work zone, or lets rivals isolate a worker, choose one consequence:

- A worker is separated and must be recovered.
- A sample case is damaged.
- A crew member suffers a minor injury.
- The next check involving labor, survey, or extraction is made with disadvantage.
- The party loses the Clean Escort Bonus if the consequence is severe or ignored.

Do not track this as a clock. Apply consequences only when the fiction earns them.

### Rift Access

The threshold is stable for the scheduled operation. The party can leave and re-enter while the Bureau intake desk keeps the permit active, unless the Warden introduces a separate table-specific reason. This adventure does not assume any collapse, break, or shutdown.

## Difficulty And Checks

Use these RA DCs throughout:

| DC | Use |
| --- | --- |
| 10 | Routine under pressure: climb broken stone, read a public Bureau notice, spot obvious marker damage. |
| 12 | Professional baseline: assess a posted hazard, calm a nervous worker, mask a minor Essence signature. |
| 15 | Dangerous field work: secure a work zone under threat, extract a core, negotiate hot contract terms. |
| 18 | Expert response: catch forged claim language, recover nearly ruined samples, hide a minor infraction from audit. |

### Common Checks

- **STR (Athletics):** brace a sample sled, pull a worker from a crack, force open a shadow-stone jam.
- **AGI (Stealth):** advance ahead without drawing anomaly attention.
- **VIT save:** resist Essence dust, cold shadow pressure, or fatigue from heavy gear.
- **INT (Investigation):** interpret survey data, compare AFA logs, find marker tampering.
- **INT (Arcana or Technology):** read Essence behavior or fix a scanner issue.
- **SENSE (Perception):** spot moving shadows, false corridors, or hidden anomaly tells.
- **PRE (Persuasion or Intimidation):** manage the crew, enforce contract boundaries, handle rivals.

### Rift Favor

Level 1 Ascendants have 3 Rift Favor points and a d4 Favor die. Available options:

- **Rift Boost:** spend 1 point to add the Favor die to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw before the Warden declares the outcome.
- **Rift Override:** spend 1 point to reroll a failed d20 and use the new result.
- **Rift Insight:** spend 1 point to learn one useful combat fact about a visible creature within 60 feet.

## Scene 1: Ironclad Contract Briefing

**Scene Type:** social, contract, role setup  
**Runtime:** 20-30 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** understand the job, negotiate terms, establish escort responsibility.

### Read-Aloud

> Ironclad Extraction Services runs out of a leased office above a parts warehouse, the kind of place where the coffee is bitter and every chair is bolted to the floor. A wall display shows a black doorway of a Rift threshold under Bureau lights. Beside it, the contract reads: Rank D, scheduled extraction, licensed escort required.
>
> Mira Voss taps the file open. "The crew does not cross without Ascendant cover. You are that cover."

### Warden Information

Mira Voss hires the party for a scheduled extraction into the Eternal Shadow Dungeon. The operation is legal, limited, and commercially routine. Ironclad wants a sample from a glassy Essence seam at Survey Marker L-3.

The party should leave this scene knowing:

- They are escorts/bodyguards, not independent looters.
- The crew depends on them for legal and physical protection.
- The Bureau has a site checkpoint and expects clean AFA logs.
- A rival contractor may have filed a nearby claim.

### Dialogue

Use or adapt these lines:

- Mira: "I am paying for judgment. If all I needed was violence, I would buy cheaper violence."
- Mira: "Tomas runs the tools. Nia runs the samples. Dev runs the survey. Rae does what Tomas tells her. You keep them alive."
- Mira: "If a rival claim agent appears, you do not punch the problem unless the problem starts punching workers."
- Tomas: "We have been inside low-rank Rifts before. We also know why we do not go without you."
- Nia: "If somebody says salvage is simple, they are selling you a bad contract."
- Rae, quietly: "Does the contract say what happens if a wall moves?"

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Why us?" | Newly licensed escorts are available, affordable, and not already tied to a larger Guild job. |
| "Is this a trap?" | Mira is worried about liability and rivals, not hiding a secret monster. |
| "Can we negotiate?" | Yes. Use PRE DC 12 for bonus timing or INT DC 12 to clarify salvage language. |
| "Can we refuse the crew?" | No. The contract is specifically escort/security for the crew. |

### Useful Checks

- **PRE (Persuasion) DC 12:** negotiate the Clean Escort Bonus up front instead of after debrief.
- **INT (Investigation) DC 12:** notice the claim language gives Ironclad rights to samples, not all salvage.
- **SENSE (Insight) DC 12:** Mira is not hiding a monster; she is worried about rival contractors.
- **INT (Bureaucracy, Law, or similar) DC 15:** identify that a rival claim could pause payment if the AFA log is sloppy.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC: "When Mira says the crew does not cross without you, what tells everyone in the room whether you take that seriously?"

### Outcomes

- **Strong opening:** the party gets a clean contract packet and a +2 circumstance bonus on one later claim dispute check.
- **Messy opening:** the contract still proceeds, but Mira insists every sample transfer be logged, adding friction in Scene 6.

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the party accepts the escort contract, understands crew protection, and knows the Bureau threshold intake is next.

### Transition

> The contract seals with a clean AFA chime. On the office wall, your names slide from pending to assigned. Tomas lifts the tool case. Nia checks the sample ledger. Rae exhales like she has been holding her breath since before you arrived.
>
> Outside, the Ironclad truck is already running.

## Scene 2: Bureau Threshold Intake

**Scene Type:** procedure, requisition, setting  
**Runtime:** 20-25 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** make the Rift Site feel official and modern.

### Read-Aloud

> The Rift Site looks less like a battlefield than a fenced work zone wrapped around something impossible. Bureau barricades mark lanes for workers, media, Guild staff, and medical response. The threshold itself hangs under white lamps: a dark, mirror-thin rupture with a rim like cold glass.
>
> A Bureau clerk scans licenses, then checks your AFA feed. "Rank D. Stable threshold. Scheduled extraction. Keep the crew inside your escort radius and do not bring me an unregistered core."

### Warden Information

The threshold is stable for scheduled access. Bureau staff are not panicked. This is a normal licensed operation, which should make the strange details feel even sharper.

### Site Advisory Handout

Give the players this:

```text
BUREAU FIELD ADVISORY
Event ID: BRO-D-GLASSLINE-L3
Site: Eternal Shadow Dungeon
Rank: D
Threshold Status: Stable for scheduled access
Contract Status: Open to licensed Ascendant escort under Ironclad Extraction Services permit
Known Activity: Shadow anomaly signatures, low-rank movement, AFA distortion in interior corridors
Notice: Unauthorized entry, unregistered harvest, or claim interference may trigger license review
```

### Requisition

The party may requisition one team support option. These are loaned for the operation and returned afterward.

- `First-Aid Trauma Kit` (`bg-trauma-kit`)
- `Field-Issue Tracking Kit` (`gap_gear_kit_common_0`)
- `Field-Issue Spyglass` (`gap_gear_utility_common_0`)
- `Dented Hard Hat` (`bg-dented-hardhat`)
- Bureau-certified Harvesting Kit, only if someone is authorized to attempt core extraction

### Dialogue

- Bureau clerk: "Hero names go in the media feed. Legal names go in the liability file. Guess which one I need."
- Bureau clerk: "If your AFA says the wall moved, log it. If the wall actually moved, step away from it and then log it."
- Dev: "I synced the survey tablet twice. It only contradicted itself once, which is better than last week."
- Nia: "Loaner gear looks clean. That means either it is new or the last team did not bring it back."
- Tomas: "Once we cross, Ascendants call security. I call tools. Nobody freelances."
- Rae: "Is everyone at the fence watching us, or does it just feel like that?"

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Can civilians enter?" | Not under this permit. Only the licensed crew under Ascendant escort. |
| "Can we leave and re-enter?" | Yes, under the active scheduled permit unless the Warden adds a table-specific complication. |
| "Can we take more gear?" | PRE DC 12 can secure one additional Common loaner item. |
| "Do we see Harlan?" | Yes on SENSE DC 10; he is near the claim board with a rival crew badge. |

### Useful Checks

- **PRE (Persuasion) DC 12:** secure one additional common loaner item.
- **INT (Technology) DC 12:** pre-sync AFA pings, gaining advantage on the first route-finding check.
- **SENSE (Perception) DC 10:** notice Harlan Pike's rival crew waiting near the claim board.
- **INT (Investigation) DC 15:** learn the rival claim overlaps the route but not the Glassline Seam itself.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC: "What does the Bureau clerk see on your license scan that makes them look twice?"

### Outcomes

- The party enters with the crew and selected requisition.
- The threshold remains available as a normal operational entrance.
- No countdown or forced collapse condition begins.

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the party selects requisition, passes license intake, and chooses marching/escort order for the crossing.

### Transition

> The clerk raises one hand. The cordon light turns green.
>
> The crew does not move until you do.
>
> The threshold waits like a black glass elevator with no buttons.

## Scene 3: First Corridor Escort

**Scene Type:** exploration plus first combat  
**Runtime:** 35-45 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** protect workers while learning the dungeon's terrain language.

### Read-Aloud

> The other side is a corridor of dark stone with reflective seams running through it like black glass. Footsteps return as delayed echoes. The crew's lights catch walls that look cut by tools, then by claws, then by something that never needed either.
>
> Dev places the first marker spike. Its green lamp blinks twice, then points in two directions at once.

### Warden Information

This scene teaches escort formation. Before any roll, ask the party how they position themselves around the crew.

Reward clear formations:

- A front guard sees the first shadow movement.
- A rear guard prevents Rae or Nia from being isolated.
- A crew-side guard can use a reaction or quick movement to intercept.
- A scout can start the fight away from the workers.

### Terrain Features

- **Shadow-stone floor:** normal movement, but running workers risk slipping.
- **Reflective seams:** bright light reveals moving silhouettes one round before an anomaly appears.
- **AFA split-ping:** devices show two possible routes until the party tests the marker path.

### Dialogue

- Dev: "The marker says left. The tablet says right. The backup tablet says both, which is not legally helpful."
- Tomas: "Nobody moves until the Ascendants say we move."
- Nia: "If something grabs the sample pack, shoot the something. Not the pack."
- Rae: "That shadow was mine a second ago. I am almost sure."
- Bureau AFA voice: "Interior interference detected. Maintain escort radius."
- Tomas, if combat starts: "Crew behind the sled. Now."

### Challenge

Useful checks:

- **SENSE (Perception) DC 12:** spot the anomaly before it reaches the workers.
- **AGI (Stealth) DC 12:** scout ahead without starting the fight near the crew.
- **PRE (Command, Persuasion, or Intimidation) DC 10:** keep the crew calm and behind cover.
- **INT (Technology) DC 12:** correct Dev's split-ping and identify the true route.

### Combat

Use the First Corridor Escort scaling table.

The anomaly's goal is not sophisticated murder. It tests motion, light, and separation. It attacks exposed targets and backs away if reduced below half HP.

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Can we avoid the fight?" | Yes, if the party spots it early and creates a protected lane; the anomaly may retreat. |
| "Can light help?" | Bright light reveals the approach vector and can justify advantage on the first prepared response. |
| "Can the crew fight?" | No. They take cover, move tools, or follow orders. |
| "Can we chase it?" | Yes, but leaving the crew exposed risks a consequence. |

### Consequences

On poor tactics or failed crew protection:

- One worker is knocked prone or separated.
- A marker spike breaks, imposing disadvantage on the next route check.
- The crew loses confidence, raising the next crew-management DC by 2.

On strong play:

- Dev's route data is clean.
- The party learns that light exposes anomaly approach vectors.
- The crew trusts the party's orders in later scenes.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC after the fight: "What did the crew just learn about how you handle danger?"

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the route is secured, the crew regroups, and Dev marks the path toward Survey Marker L-3.

### Transition

> The last echo fades. Dev's marker spike steadies, then points one way.
>
> Tomas looks at the scuffed stone, then at you. "All right. That was the hallway saying hello. Let us not make it a conversation."

## Scene 4: Survey Marker L-3

**Scene Type:** skill, escort, workspace control  
**Runtime:** 25-35 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** give the crew a safe work window.

### Read-Aloud

> Marker L-3 stands in a chamber where the floor rises in ribs of black stone. Fine glass threads grow from one wall in vertical lines. The seam is not bright. It is dark enough that your lights seem to sink into it.
>
> Tomas sets his case down and looks to you. "We need ten clean minutes."

### Warden Information

This is the first true work scene. The crew needs a protected interval to take measurements. Do not track turns unless combat begins. Ask the party for a plan:

- Who watches entrances?
- Who stays with the crew?
- Who handles AFA/scanner support?
- Who manages light and noise?

### Dialogue

- Tomas: "This is where people get sloppy. The danger gets quiet, then somebody starts acting alone."
- Dev: "Marker L-3 confirms. Survey angle is ugly, but usable."
- Nia: "I have three flasks ready. If we get four, pretend I did not say that."
- Rae: "I can hold the sled. I can hold the sled. I am saying that so my hands believe it."
- AFA feed: "Claim boundary overlay pending. Maintain field log."

### Work Window

Resolve the work with three checks. Players can choose suitable skills if they explain their action.

Suggested checks:

- **SENSE (Perception) DC 12:** watch for moving silhouettes.
- **INT (Technology or Investigation) DC 12:** keep survey readings clean.
- **PRE (Command or Persuasion) DC 10:** keep the crew calm and efficient.
- **STR (Athletics) DC 12:** brace the sample sled on uneven stone.
- **INT (Arcana) DC 15:** identify the seam's safest sample points.

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Can we block entrances?" | Yes. Good positioning can grant advantage on the Perception or crew-management check. |
| "Can Dev help us?" | Yes, if protected; Dev can provide advantage on one route or seam-related check. |
| "Can we move the crew back?" | Yes, but it slows work and may reduce the survey result by one success. |
| "Can we take samples now?" | Only small survey flakes; extraction happens in Scene 6. |

### Results

- **3 successes:** clean survey. Grant advantage on one Scene 6 extraction check.
- **2 successes:** usable survey. No bonus or penalty.
- **1 success:** rushed survey. Scene 6 starts with one damaged tool or frightened worker.
- **0 successes:** incomplete survey. Ironclad can still proceed, but the documentation bonus is at risk.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC: "What small professional detail do you catch that keeps the work from going wrong?"

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the survey result is known and the crew is ready to proceed toward the Glassline Seam.

### Transition

> Dev saves the survey file. The AFA feed accepts it, hesitates, then accepts it again.
>
> From the corridor behind you, another set of work lights appears.

> A voice calls out, too calm for the place: "Nobody touches that seam yet."

## Scene 5: Rival Claim Dispute

**Scene Type:** social/legal, optional chase or nonlethal conflict  
**Runtime:** 30-40 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** resolve a claim dispute without tanking the party's license record.

### Read-Aloud

> Voices carry through the corridor before the rival lights appear. Harlan Pike steps into view with two workers behind him and a claim tablet already raised.
>
> "Ironclad's permit covers approach and survey. The seam crosses a prior file. Nobody touches that glass until we verify ownership."

### Warden Information

Harlan's claim is partly true and mostly opportunistic. His crew has a nearby route claim, but not extraction rights to the Glassline Seam. He wants delay, leverage, or a concession.

This is not meant to become a lethal fight. The best outcomes come from documentation, negotiation, or exposing his overreach.

### Dialogue

- Harlan: "I am not accusing anyone. Accusations are for people with weak paperwork."
- Mira over AFA, if contacted: "Do not let him bait you. Ask for the boundary timestamp."
- Tomas: "Harlan Pike. Of course. I was hoping the Rift would be the annoying part."
- Nia: "If he touches my sample labels, I become less charming."
- Dev: "His overlay is using an old route layer. I think. Please let that be true."
- Rae, low: "Are we allowed to tell him to leave, or is that a form?"

### Flexible Resolution

Support any reasonable plan that proves the claim, lowers tension, or moves the crew safely. Do not require one exact solution.

- **Contract proof:** INT (Investigation) DC 12 to compare claim boundaries.
- **Legal pressure:** PRE (Persuasion or Intimidation) DC 15 to force Harlan to accept AFA arbitration.
- **Technical proof:** INT (Technology) DC 12 to overlay AFA route logs.
- **Worker solidarity:** PRE (Persuasion) DC 12 to convince Harlan's workers not to escalate.
- **Low-profile bypass:** AGI (Stealth) DC 15 to move the crew around the disputed corridor without giving Harlan a clean complaint.
- **Compromise:** offer Harlan a later secondary sample notice, preserving Ironclad's primary claim.

### If Things Escalate

Use a chase, shoving match, or disarm scene before combat. If the party attacks first, Harlan withdraws and files a complaint. This can cost the documentation bonus or create a future rival, but it does not stop the one-shot.

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Is Harlan lying?" | He is exaggerating an old route overlap into a seam dispute. |
| "Can we call the Bureau?" | Yes. AFA arbitration supports the party if their logs are clean. |
| "Can we intimidate him?" | Yes, but threats against licensed workers create debrief risk. |
| "Can we make a deal?" | Yes. A future secondary sample notice is enough to let him save face. |

### Outcomes

- **Clean win:** the party protects Ironclad's claim and gains +500 credits per Ascendant or advantage on debrief reputation.
- **Compromise:** Ironclad keeps the seam, but Harlan gets access to a later secondary sample. No bonus.
- **Messy win:** the party keeps the seam but creates a rival claim cost in debrief.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC: "When Harlan tries to make the party look like amateurs, how do you answer without losing control of the room?"

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the rival crew leaves, accepts arbitration, follows a compromise, or withdraws to file a complaint.

### Transition

> Harlan's lights recede down the corridor, but his last look stays behind.
>
> Nia seals the sample ledger with a thumbprint. "Good news. We still have the claim."
>
> Dev looks toward the seam chamber. "Bad news. The readings got louder while we argued."

## Scene 6: The Glassline Seam

**Scene Type:** extraction, tools, risk choice  
**Runtime:** 35-45 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** protect the crew while they extract registered samples.

### Read-Aloud

> The seam opens into a narrow chamber where black crystal grows from the wall in panes thin enough to ring when the air moves. Nia unwraps stabilization flasks. Dev's scanner fills with numbers, then warnings, then numbers again.
>
> The crew looks at you before anyone starts cutting.

### Warden Information

The extraction is the party's main risk-choice scene. They are not racing a timer. They are choosing how much reward pressure they can handle while keeping workers safe.

### Dialogue

- Tomas: "Call it. Safe, standard, or greedy. I can do all three. I only recommend two."
- Nia: "For the record, I hate the word greedy. For the off-record, I brought extra labels."
- Dev: "If the scanner squeals, stop cutting. If I squeal, also stop cutting."
- Rae: "I can brace the sled from here. Somebody tell me this is a good spot."
- AFA feed: "Sample integrity channel open. Chain of custody recording."

### Extraction Procedure

The party chooses one approach:

1. **Safe sampling:** lower reward risk, lower complication risk.
2. **Standard extraction:** normal reward and normal risk.
3. **Aggressive pull:** better chance at bonus salvage, higher chance of injury, damaged gear, or anomaly attention.

This is not a clock. Resolve the chosen approach with checks and consequences.

### Safe Sampling

Requires two successes before one failure.

- DC 10 with appropriate tools.
- Produces enough sample for contract completion.
- Cannot earn the uncommon item bonus by itself.

### Standard Extraction

Requires three successes before two failures.

- DC 12 for tool, survey, and escort actions.
- Produces contract sample and normal salvage.
- Qualifies for the uncommon item bonus if the rival dispute was cleanly resolved.

### Aggressive Pull

Requires three successes before one failure.

- DC 15 for most checks.
- On success, qualifies for uncommon item bonus and a stronger debrief outcome.
- On failure, choose two costs: worker injury, ruined sample, damaged gear, immediate final encounter with poor positioning, or documentation bonus at risk.

### Useful Checks

- **STR (Athletics) DC 12/15:** hold the sled or brace a crystal pane.
- **AGI (Sleight of Hand) DC 12/15:** guide extraction tools without cracking the seam.
- **INT (Technology) DC 12/15:** keep scanner data clean.
- **INT (Arcana) DC 12/15:** identify a resonance surge before it hits.
- **SENSE (Perception) DC 12:** spot the guardian's shadow approaching.
- **PRE (Command) DC 10/12:** coordinate crew timing.

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Can we stop after one success?" | Yes. Treat it as Safe Sampling with reduced reward ceiling. |
| "Can powers or gear help?" | Yes, if the player explains how it protects crew, tools, samples, or readings. |
| "Can we move samples first?" | Yes. It may protect the documentation bonus but slows extraction. |
| "Can Nia or Dev roll?" | They can assist if protected, granting advantage or lowering a DC by 2 at Warden discretion. |

### Legal Harvest Option

If an anomaly dies in this scene or the next, a character with a Bureau-certified Harvesting Kit may attempt the RA harvesting procedure:

- **Time:** 1 minute uninterrupted.
- **Check:** DC 15 INT (Medicine or Survival).
- **Success:** one registered Rank-D Essence core, placed in a stabilization flask.
- **Failure:** the core degrades into useless ash.
- **Critical failure:** Essence Detonation, dealing force damage equal to the anomaly's average damage output to everyone within 10 feet; the core is destroyed.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC: "When the seam begins to ring, what do you do that makes the crew keep working instead of freezing?"

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when the sample is secured, ruined, or abandoned, and the Claim Guardian reveals itself or blocks the route out.

### Transition

> The final flask seals with a hiss.
>
> For one clean second, everyone breathes.
>
> Then every shadow in the chamber points at the sample sled.

## Scene 7: Claim Guardian

**Scene Type:** final combat  
**Runtime:** 40-55 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** complete the escort contract and leave with workers and samples intact.

### Read-Aloud

> The glassline stops ringing all at once.
>
> Every shadow in the chamber bends toward the seam. One of them pulls itself upright, too tall at first, then folding down into a shape that almost understands arms, shoulders, and a face.
>
> Dev whispers, "That was not on the route scan."

### Warden Information

Use the Claim Guardian scaling table. The guardian blocks the extraction route, threatens the sample sled, and attacks anyone carrying bright lights or Essence samples. It should feel like a tactical problem around worker safety, not a static HP bag.

### Dialogue

- Tomas: "Crew behind the sled. Ascendants, tell us where to move."
- Nia: "It wants the samples. I am choosing not to take that personally."
- Dev: "Its signature is inside the seam reflection. It is not entering. It was already here."
- Rae: "I am moving when you say move. Please say it loudly."
- Harlan over distant AFA, optional if he stayed nearby: "Ironclad, your feed just spiked. Do you require witness support or should I pretend I did not see that?"
- Mira over AFA, if signal holds: "Bring me people first. Samples second. Pride last."

### Battlefield Features

- **Sample sled:** provides half cover to a crouching worker; if damaged twice, the documentation bonus is at risk.
- **Glassline panes:** AC 10, fragile; breaking one creates difficult terrain in a 10-foot square.
- **Bright work lights:** a character can use an object interaction to redirect light. The first time each round a Shadow anomaly enters a lit lane, the next attack against it before the start of its next turn gains advantage.
- **Worker panic:** if a worker is hit or isolated, a PC can use an action or bonus action with PRE DC 10 to keep them from running into danger.

### Enemy Goals

- Attack the closest isolated target.
- Push workers away from Ascendants.
- Break lights or threaten the sample sled when ignored.
- Withdraw into shadow if the party retreats with the crew and samples.

### Flexible Victory

The party succeeds if they do one of the following:

- Defeat all hostile anomalies.
- Drive the guardian away and withdraw with crew and samples.
- Distract the guardian long enough for workers to leave.
- Use light, positioning, and anomaly behavior to create a safe exit route.

Do not require killing every creature if the party completes the escort objective.

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Can we run?" | Yes. If the party protects the crew and samples, retreat can be success. |
| "Can we sacrifice the samples?" | Yes. It likely saves lives but changes debrief and rewards. |
| "Can light hold it back?" | Light does not end the fight by itself, but it helps expose and punish movement. |
| "Can we harvest it?" | Yes, after it is slain and if a character has the proper kit and 1 uninterrupted minute. |

### Defeat Or Retreat

If the party retreats without samples but keeps the crew alive, the contract is a partial success. Pay the base amount, no salvage parcel, and give Ironclad a future chance to rehire them.

If a worker dies, the one-shot should still finish, but debrief becomes serious: Bureau review, reduced pay, no clean professional reputation, and a harder Run Silent handoff.

### Player Spotlight

Ask one PC near the end of the fight: "What does your finishing move, rescue, command, or sacrifice look like on the AFA footage?"

### Exit Condition

The scene ends when hostile anomalies are defeated, driven off, or bypassed and the party has a clear route back to the threshold.

### Transition

> The corridor back looks different with the sample case in hand. Narrower. Longer. Quieter.
>
> Then the Bureau threshold lights appear ahead, pale and ordinary and beautiful.

## Scene 8: Debrief And Carryover

**Scene Type:** aftermath, rewards, campaign hook  
**Runtime:** 20-30 minutes  
**Primary Stakes:** lock in rewards and decide what follows the party.

### Read-Aloud

> Bureau floodlights look almost gentle after the dark. The threshold hangs behind you, ordinary in the way impossible things become ordinary when enough people put clipboards around them.
>
> Mira Voss takes the sample case, checks the seals, and looks past it to the crew. "Everybody out?"

### Dialogue

- Mira, if clean success: "That is what professional looks like. I will be writing exactly that."
- Mira, if messy success: "You brought them back. We will discuss everything else after the medic signs off."
- Bureau clerk: "Registered samples, attached logs, no missing workers. I am almost disappointed."
- Tomas: "You ever need someone to say you did the job, send them to me."
- Nia: "The samples are ugly, legal, and valuable. My three favorite qualities."
- Rae: "I thought I would hate crossing back more. I do not. I really, really do not."
- Harlan, if present: "You are less careless than I expected. That is not praise. Do not write it down."

### If Players Ask

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "Do we level up?" | Default is no; the party stays level 1 and carries rewards forward. If the table wants Run Silent to start at level 2, award a milestone only after full contract success. |
| "Can we keep the loaned gear?" | No. Loaned requisition gear is returned, but awarded salvage items are registered to the party. |
| "Can we work for Ironclad again?" | Yes. Clean or messy success can both create future contract offers, but the tone of the offer changes. |
| "Does this reveal Run Silent?" | No. It creates a professional reason for the party's names to surface later without importing campaign rules, region lore, or spoilers. |

### Debrief Questions

Ask and answer these at the table:

- Did every crew member return alive?
- Did the party preserve samples and AFA logs?
- Was the rival claim resolved cleanly?
- Was any Essence core legally registered?
- Did the party create a Bureau complaint, rival grudge, or public reputation?

### Debrief Outcomes

| Outcome | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Clean Professional Success | full pay, salvage parcel, minor Ironclad/Guild favor, clean Run Silent handoff |
| Profitable But Messy | full or partial pay, salvage parcel, rival claim or Bureau note follows them |
| Crew Saved, Samples Lost | base pay only, minor goodwill, no salvage parcel |
| Samples Saved, Crew Harmed | reduced pay, Bureau review, no bonus, future complication |
| Contract Failure | no bonus, possible partial base pay, Ironclad distrust, but survival still matters |

### Run Silent Handoff

Do not reveal or use Run Silent-specific rules here. Instead, use one of these clean bridges:

- **Professional Referral:** Ironclad forwards the party's names as reliable low-rank escorts for a future Bureau-adjacent first-entry operation.
- **Bureau Notice:** the party's AFA logs show unusually disciplined escort behavior, drawing a quiet request for interview and availability.
- **Rival Thread:** Harlan Pike files a complaint or begrudging commendation, putting the party's names into a contractor review pool.
- **Salvage Lead:** a registered sample contains a routing note tied to a later campaign contract, not a special rule or immediate revelation.

The hook should move the party toward Run Silent without placing this one-shot in that campaign's region and without importing its mechanics.

### Player Spotlight

Ask each PC one quick closing question:

- What part of the job are you proud of?
- What part are you not saying out loud?
- Which NPC now has an opinion about you?
- What reward do you check first when the payout clears?

### Exit Condition

The adventure ends when payout, salvage, favor, optional harvest, and campaign handoff are recorded.

### Transition

Use this transition if the table is continuing into Run Silent. Keep it administrative and professional: the party does not learn campaign-region lore, does not trigger new mechanics, and does not discover a hidden truth about this Rift. Their clean escort record simply makes them visible to people who hire reliable Ascendants.

### Closing Image

> The Glassline file closes. Your payment clears. The AFA archive marks your crew escort as complete.
>
> A new notation appears beside your names:
>
> `LOW-RANK ESCORT PERFORMANCE - RELIABLE`
>
> The system files it away where someone else can find it later.
