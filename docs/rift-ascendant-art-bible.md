# Rift Ascendant Art Bible

## Source Priority

Use `docs/rift-ascendant-world-lore.md` as the global visual source of truth. For Gloamreach campaign assets, the current sandbox module text for `Run Silent` and `The Quiet` is the latest canon: the Gloamreach is a survival and psychological horror Interior, and the Quiet is the unseen apex predator that hunts by noise, light, and Essence use.

## 1. Core Identity

Rift Ascendant is modern supernatural urban fantasy in the early, normalized Rift Age. It is not post-apocalyptic. Cities still function. People still use phones, hospitals, rent, media, celebrity culture, sponsors, laws, guild contracts, Bureau clearances, Essence markets, and Rift alerts.

The visual identity should always hold that contrast:

- Material world: modern, commercial, urban, alive, Bureau-controlled, media-aware.
- Rift Thresholds: impossible ruptures intruding into normal infrastructure.
- Rift Interiors and Domains: uncanny, dangerous, surreal, predatory, rule-bending, and often dark fantasy horror.

## 2. Illustration Style

Use a high-detail fantasy game illustration style that feels adjacent to premium tabletop RPG key art and Korean action webtoon dark fantasy without copying a named franchise or specific copyrighted characters.

Preferred style language:

- High-detail tabletop fantasy RPG key art.
- Korean action webtoon-inspired dark fantasy rendering.
- Dynamic composition, crisp ink-like edges, painterly realism, cinematic contrast, and dramatic rim lighting.
- Polished game illustration with strong silhouettes and clear foreground/background separation.
- Modern supernatural urban fantasy materials: tactical streetwear, Bureau gear, Essence batteries, scanners, Relics, contemporary weapons, and Rift-touched surfaces.
- Black, deep blue, violet, silver, pale cyan, rift-white highlights, and restrained crimson accents.

Do not put direct franchise style requests, official logos, trademarked symbols, celebrity likenesses, or copyrighted characters into generation prompts. Translate references such as tabletop fantasy RPG art or Korean action webtoon energy into the descriptive style language above.

## 3. Material-World Image Language

Material-world assets should look like a normal modern society that has adapted to impossible doors.

Use:

- Modern city lights, street grids, offices, apartments, subway stations, hospitals, ports, rooftops, and highways.
- Bureau barricades, emergency cordons, medical tents, police lines, field scanners, evacuation signage, containment rigs, and inspection lights.
- Media crews, drones, guild staging areas, branded Ascendant teams, contractors, sponsors, and crowd control.
- Contemporary Ascendant gear: tactical jackets, reinforced streetwear, smart devices, AFA screens, earpieces, visors, resonance scanners, Essence batteries, and compact modern weapons.
- Rift Thresholds as glowing ruptures, mirror-dark doorways, distortions, impossible openings, black seams in concrete, or luminous geometry that should not fit the space.

Avoid making the outside world look like a dead wasteland. A Rift Site can be dangerous and restricted without looking like civilization has ended.

## 4. Rift Interior Image Language

Rift Interiors are where the setting becomes strange. They can contain ruins, weather, roads, ecology, native settlements, impossible buildings, and hostile rules, but they should not default to generic medieval fantasy unless the Interior specifically calls for it.

Use:

- Impossible spaces and distorted scale.
- Wrong roads, false horizons, repeated architecture, rooms that remember, and maps that cannot be trusted.
- Hostile weather, light that behaves incorrectly, living ruins, strange ecology, and Essence glow.
- Anomalies as creatures, hazards, constructs, parasites, memory echoes, swarms, living weapons, or entities following laws humans do not understand.
- Relics and Essence as useful, dangerous, valuable, and sometimes cursed or classified.

Do not use generic fantasy taverns, castles, dragons, or goblins unless the source asset has that exact context.

## 5. Gloamreach Image Language

The Gloamreach is an S-Rank, country-sized Rift Interior. The world lore describes roads, settlements, ruins, mills, warrens, predator ranges, a fallen bastion, a forbidden vault, and a citadel visible from almost everywhere. Current `Run Silent` canon reframes the campaign around the Quiet: an unseen apex predator that hunts by noise, light, and Essence, wears the dead, and is almost never safely seen.

Use these motifs for Gloamreach assets:

- Roads that watch, lengthen, bend, and punish noise.
- Warded native safe-holds: tense normalcy, curfews, offerings, wardlines, dim interiors, people surviving by rules.
- Exposed hunting ground: open dark, bad silence, dead roads, lights hidden or dangerous.
- Drowned hospital-fen and flooded clinical memory.
- Rot caverns, damp stone, fungal ruin, sleeping faces in decay.
- Memory orchard, stolen faces, fruit or branches carrying grief and recollection.
- Fallen bastion and forbidden vault as places of old defense and worse knowledge.
- Worn dead, lures, the Caller, the Wrong Shape, the Hollowed, and other almost-human threats.
- The Quiet as pressure and presence more than a displayed monster: patient, vast, hungry, old, and almost unseen.

When older Regent imagery appears, treat the Regent language as domain authority and Anchor horror unless a current `Run Silent` asset clearly points to the Quiet instead. The Quiet should feel unavoidable without becoming a generic boss creature.

## 6. UI/App Asset Rules

Assets must work inside the app, not only as full-size illustrations.

- Strong silhouette first.
- High readability at card size and token size.
- Avoid tiny decorative detail on icons.
- Avoid baked-in text, captions, logos, watermarks, UI chrome, or fake app labels.
- Leave safe negative space for overlays, titles, badges, and cards.
- Prefer clean foreground/background separation.
- Character portraits need readable faces and contemporary clothing or gear unless lore says otherwise.
- Icons and powers need centered, high-contrast shapes.
- Maps and backgrounds need coherent composition and should not hide important UI under visual clutter.

Match the existing Ascendant UI style: dark panels, high contrast, sharp supernatural highlights, and clean app readability.

## 7. Consistency Rules

Default model stack:

- Required default: `stabilityai/stable-diffusion-xl-base-1.0`
- Expected identity: SDXL Base 1.0
- Optional refinement: `stabilityai/stable-diffusion-xl-refiner-1.0`

Use the same negative prompt bank across assets:

- No placeholder image.
- No stock photo look.
- No generic medieval-only fantasy unless explicitly supported.
- No unrelated sci-fi, spaceship, or full cyberpunk overload.
- No chibi, childish cartoon, joke creature, celebrity likeness, copyrighted character, direct franchise imitation, text, logo, watermark, signature, or low-resolution artifacts.
- No bad anatomy, extra fingers, missing fingers, distorted faces, or muddy silhouettes.

Color motifs:

- Black
- Deep blue
- Violet
- Silver
- Pale cyan
- Rift-white
- Faint crimson for danger, blood memory, Quiet pressure, or Regent horror

Use neon as an accent, especially in material-world Bureau, guild, AFA, and city scenes. Avoid turning the whole setting into neon cyberpunk.
