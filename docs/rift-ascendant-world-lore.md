# Rift Ascendant Gate Domain Lore

## Source Authority

This document is the campaign-facing lore source for The Shadow of the Regent. It defines how high-rank Rifts, Gate Domains, Anchors, pantheon signatures, and Regent law work for this module.

## The Remade World

The Remade World is a modern world of Sanctuary Cities, Bureau cordons, Ascendant guilds, Essence markets, arcology transit, mana-lattice infrastructure, and threshold zones shaped by Rift response doctrine.

The material side is where people live, trade, license Ascendants, argue over contracts, and wait under the light of a Rift. The Gate Domain is where the horror happens.

## Rifts, Gates, and Gate Domains

A Rift is the breach. A Gate is the stabilized threshold. A Gate Domain is the place beyond it.

Most low-rank Gates open into limited chambers, short ruin-complexes, forests, tunnels, labs, or pocket battlefields. High-rank Gates can open into whole interior realms: valleys with weather, roads, settlements, citadels, dead seas, predator forests, buried archives, or chained regions that obey a hostile logic.

The Gate is not the adventure location. It is the door.

The Domain beyond the door can be large enough to trap armies.

## Anchors

Every persistent Gate Domain has an Anchor. The Anchor keeps the threshold stable and gives the Domain its laws.

Common Anchors include:

- a ruling Anomaly intelligence;
- a Regent;
- a Relic engine;
- a domain heart;
- an Essence furnace;
- a bound pantheon signature;
- a living ecology that has become self-aware.

Clearing a Gate means breaking, sealing, transforming, or extracting the Anchor. Killing every creature inside a Domain is not enough if the Anchor remains intact.

## Blue Phase and Red Phase

Blue Phase means the Gate is open but contained. The Bureau can measure the threshold, issue Rank clearance, deploy Ascendants, and maintain a cordon.

Red Phase means containment has failed. The Gate Domain begins to overwrite the material side. Weather crosses first, then terrain, then Anomalies, then the Anchor's law. This event is a Gate Break.

The Bureau does not call a Gate safe because it understands it. The Bureau calls it safe while its instruments still have numbers.

## Ascendants and Bureau Classification

Ascendants are people whose mana-lattice resonance lets them survive and act inside Gate Domains. Their Jobs, Paths, Ranks, and clearances are Bureau classifications over observable phenomena.

The AFA is a device and record layer. It reports resonance, Rank estimates, Gate timers, Anchor instability, injury data, contract status, and Bureau warnings. It does not grant destiny.

## Pantheon Signatures

The Eternals and Exarchs rarely appear as people inside a campaign. They leave signatures.

- Xylo appears in archives, impossible ledgers, preserved truths, and records that survive destruction.
- Cipher appears in cold judgment, crystalline stillness, and truths held in absolute silence.
- Lyra appears in mutation, swarms, predatory adaptation, and Gate ecologies that learn.
- Sylph appears in broken distance, moving thresholds, impossible roads, and wind through sealed rooms.
- Nyx appears in ambush, hunger, pack law, and the moment Rank fails before instinct.
- Marthos appears in deletion, rot, failed containment, and the mercy of ending what cannot be saved.
- Solara appears in shields, triage light, last stands, and protection that burns the protector.
- Golem appears in walls, foundations, bastions, and the refusal of a line to fall.
- Vaelen appears in trials, battle doctrine, escalating challenge, and war made ritual.
- Elara appears in beauty made unbearable, art that keeps survivors human, and comfort twisted into bait.
- Kronos appears in clocks, thresholds, sequence, and consequences that arrive exactly when promised.
- Kael Voss appears in umbral command, extracted echoes, and sovereign power that refuses collapse.

In the Gloamreach, pantheon signatures are environmental pressure rather than divine visitation. Xylo clings to Bureau ledgers that should have burned. Cipher hardens the Black Vault into silent judgment. Sylph bends the Gallows Road. Marthos stains the Essence Mill and Red Phase mercy-horror. Solara and Golem linger in last stands at the Bastion, Aegis Hollow, and the White Heron Reliquary. Vaelen marks trial roads and Citadel challenge rites. Elara turns hospitality into bait. Kronos measures the Blue Phase clock and Citadel sequences. Kael Voss echoes through the Regent's sovereignty, umbral command, and refusal to collapse.

## Regents

A Regent is not merely a powerful Anomaly. A Regent is domain authority with will. When a Regent becomes the Anchor of a Gate Domain, the land itself acts like a body and a court.

Regents do not simply wait in final rooms. They send invitations, alter roads, bargain through Relics, punish settlements, elevate servants, and study intruders. Their horror is not size. It is ownership.

## Subject Zero

Subject Zero is an older sealed S-Rank Anomaly whose pressure leaks into some high-rank Domains. It is not the Regent. It is not loyal to the Regent. It is an outside hunger that can offer power through forbidden bargains.

When Subject Zero touches a Domain, the bargain should be real, useful, and costly.

## The Gloamreach

The Gloamreach is the S-Rank Gate Domain of The Shadow of the Regent. It is a country-sized interior realm behind one Rift. It has roads, settlements, ruins, mills, warrens, predator ranges, a fallen bastion, a forbidden vault, and a citadel visible from almost everywhere.

The Regent is the Gloamreach's Anchor.

The material side matters only as the place the party leaves, the place the Gate may break into, and the place that must live with the ending.

## Campaign Tone

The Shadow of the Regent is hard horror inside a Gate Domain:

- scarcity without mercy;
- settlements surviving by tribute;
- Ascendants reduced from elite operators to trapped intruders;
- Bureau failure recorded in clean language;
- Vermillion pragmatism that saves and exploits;
- Anomalies as ecology, punishment, livestock, and law;
- Relics that solve problems by teaching the party to think like the Domain;
- a Regent who is charismatic, predatory, and patient.

The campaign should feel modern in tools and institutions, but the adventure space is the Gate Domain. Roads should be wrong. Weather should judge. Safe places should feel leased, not owned. Every clear should ask what law the party broke to survive.
